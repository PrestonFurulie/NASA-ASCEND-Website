About ASCEND

The NASA ASCEND (Aerospace Student Collaboration & Exploration with Near-space Data) internship program is a prestigious high-altitude ballooning initiative funded by the Arizona/NASA Space Grant Consortium. This program provides exceptional opportunities for undergraduate STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) students to apply theoretical knowledge to real-world aerospace challenges. ASCEND participants design, construct, and deploy sophisticated scientific payloads that soar to the edge of space, capturing invaluable atmospheric data while gaining professional experience in mission planning, systems engineering, and collaborative research. Students work alongside NASA professionals and industry experts, developing critical skills in aerospace engineering, data analysis, project management, and scientific communication that prepare them for successful careers in the space industry and beyond.

Stratospheric Research Missions

Engineer and deploy advanced scientific payloads on high-altitude weather balloons reaching altitudes exceeding 100,000 feet. Collect critical atmospheric data including temperature, pressure, UV radiation, and capture stunning imagery from the edge of space. Each mission provides hands-on experience in payload integration, telemetry systems, and recovery operations.

Collaborative Engineering Teams

Join a diverse network of over 100 Arizona STEM students from colleges and universities across the state. Work in cross-functional teams on mission-critical tasks including systems design, electrical engineering, programming, data analysis, and mission operations. Develop essential teamwork and leadership skills valued by aerospace industry employers.

Professional Career Development

Gain competitive advantage through authentic NASA mission experience. Build a professional portfolio with real aerospace engineering projects, develop technical documentation skills, present research findings, and network with NASA professionals and industry leaders. ASCEND alumni have successfully transitioned to careers at NASA, aerospace contractors, and leading technology companies.

Mission Teams & Alumni

Celebrating the exceptional students, mentors, and faculty who have successfully launched over 12 high-altitude balloon missions, advancing atmospheric research and inspiring the next generation of aerospace engineers.
